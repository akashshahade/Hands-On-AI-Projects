# Sample Workout Plans

## Example 1: Beginner Full Body Workout (3 Days/Week)

### Overview
This beginner-friendly program focuses on building foundational strength and proper movement patterns using bodyweight exercises. Perfect for those new to fitness or returning after a break.

### Weekly Schedule
- **Monday**: Full Body Workout A
- **Wednesday**: Full Body Workout B  
- **Friday**: Full Body Workout C

### Day 1: Full Body Workout A

**Warm-up (5-10 minutes)**
- Jumping jacks: 2 minutes
- Arm circles: 30 seconds each direction
- Leg swings: 10 each leg
- Bodyweight squats: 10 reps

**Main Workout**

1. **Push-ups (or Knee Push-ups)**
   - Sets: 3
   - Reps: 8-12
   - Rest: 60 seconds
   - Notes: Keep core tight, lower until chest nearly touches ground

2. **Bodyweight Squats**
   - Sets: 3
   - Reps: 12-15
   - Rest: 60 seconds
   - Notes: Push hips back, keep chest up, knees track over toes

3. **Plank Hold**
   - Sets: 3
   - Duration: 30-45 seconds
   - Rest: 60 seconds
   - Notes: Keep body in straight line, engage core

4. **Lunges**
   - Sets: 3
   - Reps: 10 each leg
   - Rest: 60 seconds
   - Notes: Keep front knee behind toes, drop back knee toward ground

5. **Mountain Climbers**
   - Sets: 3
   - Duration: 30 seconds
   - Rest: 60 seconds
   - Notes: Keep hips low, drive knees to chest

**Cool-down (5-10 minutes)**
- Child's pose: 1 minute
- Standing quad stretch: 30 seconds each leg
- Hamstring stretch: 30 seconds each leg
- Deep breathing: 2 minutes

## Example 2: Intermediate Gym Workout (4 Days/Week)

### Overview
Progressive push/pull/legs split focused on muscle building with compound movements and proper volume.

### Weekly Schedule
- **Monday**: Push (Chest, Shoulders, Triceps)
- **Tuesday**: Pull (Back, Biceps)
- **Thursday**: Legs
- **Saturday**: Upper Body

### Day 1: Push Day

**Warm-up (10 minutes)**
- 5 minutes treadmill
- Arm circles and shoulder rotations
- Light cable flyes: 2 sets of 15

**Main Workout**

1. **Barbell Bench Press**
   - Sets: 4
   - Reps: 8-10
   - Rest: 90 seconds
   - Notes: Lower to mid-chest, press straight up

2. **Incline Dumbbell Press**
   - Sets: 3
   - Reps: 10-12
   - Rest: 75 seconds
   - Notes: 30-45 degree incline, full range of motion

3. **Overhead Dumbbell Press**
   - Sets: 3
   - Reps: 10-12
   - Rest: 75 seconds
   - Notes: Press straight overhead, avoid arching back

4. **Cable Lateral Raises**
   - Sets: 3
   - Reps: 12-15
   - Rest: 60 seconds
   - Notes: Slight bend in elbows, raise to shoulder height

5. **Tricep Rope Pushdowns**
   - Sets: 3
   - Reps: 12-15
   - Rest: 60 seconds
   - Notes: Keep elbows at sides, full extension

6. **Cable Flyes**
   - Sets: 3
   - Reps: 12-15
   - Rest: 60 seconds
   - Notes: Slight bend in elbows, squeeze at center

**Cool-down**
- Chest stretch: 1 minute
- Shoulder stretches: 1 minute
- Tricep stretch: 30 seconds each arm

## Example 3: Weight Loss HIIT Program (4 Days/Week)

### Overview
High-intensity interval training combined with strength work for maximum calorie burn and fat loss.

### Weekly Schedule
- **Monday**: HIIT & Lower Body
- **Wednesday**: HIIT & Upper Body
- **Friday**: Full Body HIIT
- **Saturday**: Active Recovery/Cardio

### Day 1: HIIT & Lower Body

**Warm-up (5 minutes)**
- Light jogging: 2 minutes
- Dynamic stretches: 3 minutes

**HIIT Circuit (20 minutes)**
*Perform each exercise for 40 seconds, rest 20 seconds*
*Complete 4 rounds*

1. Burpees
2. Jump squats
3. High knees
4. Mountain climbers
5. Jump lunges

**Strength Circuit (20 minutes)**

1. **Dumbbell Goblet Squats**
   - Sets: 4
   - Reps: 12-15
   - Rest: 45 seconds

2. **Romanian Deadlifts**
   - Sets: 4
   - Reps: 10-12
   - Rest: 45 seconds

3. **Walking Lunges**
   - Sets: 3
   - Reps: 12 each leg
   - Rest: 45 seconds

4. **Leg Press or Wall Sit**
   - Sets: 3
   - Reps: 15 or 45 seconds
   - Rest: 45 seconds

**Cool-down (5 minutes)**
- Walking: 2 minutes
- Static stretches: 3 minutes

## General Guidelines

### Progression
- Increase weight when you can complete all sets with good form
- Add 2.5-5 lbs for upper body exercises
- Add 5-10 lbs for lower body exercises
- Increase reps before increasing weight

### Recovery
- Sleep 7-9 hours per night
- Stay hydrated (half your body weight in ounces)
- Eat protein within 2 hours post-workout
- Active recovery on rest days (walking, yoga, stretching)

### Safety Tips
- Always warm up properly
- Use proper form over heavy weight
- Stop if you feel sharp pain
- Progress gradually
- Listen to your body

### Tracking Progress
- Log workouts (weights, reps, sets)
- Take measurements every 2 weeks
- Progress photos every 4 weeks
- Track how you feel and energy levels
- Monitor strength improvements
