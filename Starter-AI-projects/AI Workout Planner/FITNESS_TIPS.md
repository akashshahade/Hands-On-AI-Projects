## üéØ Goal-Specific Guidelines

### Building Muscle (Hypertrophy)

**Training Focus:**
- 8-12 reps per set for most exercises
- 3-5 sets per exercise
- Progressive overload is key
- 60-90 seconds rest between sets
- Train each muscle group 2x per week

**Nutrition:**
- Calorie surplus of 200-500 calories
- 1.6-2.2g protein per kg body weight
- Eat protein every 3-4 hours
- Don't skip carbs - they fuel growth
- Stay hydrated (3-4 liters water/day)

**Recovery:**
- 7-9 hours sleep nightly
- Rest day between training same muscle
- Consider deload week every 4-6 weeks

### Losing Weight (Fat Loss)

**Training Focus:**
- Mix of cardio and strength training
- Higher rep ranges (12-15+)
- Shorter rest periods (30-45 seconds)
- Include HIIT 2-3x per week
- Maintain strength training to preserve muscle

**Nutrition:**
- Calorie deficit of 300-500 calories
- High protein (1.8-2.4g per kg)
- Prioritize whole foods
- Track your food intake
- Meal prep for success

**Cardio Strategy:**
- LISS (Low-Intensity Steady State): 30-45 min, 3-4x/week
- HIIT: 15-20 min, 2-3x/week
- Mix both for best results
- Don't overdo cardio - preserve muscle

### Increasing Strength

**Training Focus:**
- Lower reps (3-6) with heavy weight
- Longer rest (2-5 minutes)
- Focus on compound movements
- Progressive overload mandatory
- Train main lifts 2x per week

**Key Exercises:**
- Squat, Deadlift, Bench Press, Overhead Press
- These build overall strength
- Master form before adding weight

**Programming:**
- Linear progression for beginners
- Periodization for intermediate/advanced
- Track all lifts in a logbook
- Deload every 4 weeks

### Improving Endurance

**Training Focus:**
- Higher rep ranges (15-20+)
- Minimal rest (30 seconds or less)
- Circuit training effective
- Progressive distance/time increase
- Mix cardio types

**Workout Types:**
- Steady-state cardio: Build base
- Interval training: Improve VO2 max
- Tempo runs: Lactate threshold
- Long slow distance: Aerobic capacity

## üèãÔ∏è Exercise Form Essentials

### Squat Form
1. Feet shoulder-width apart
2. Toes slightly pointed out
3. Push hips back first
4. Keep chest up, core tight
5. Knees track over toes
6. Depth: hip crease below knee
7. Drive through heels to stand

### Deadlift Form
1. Bar over mid-foot
2. Shoulder blades over bar
3. Neutral spine (no rounding)
4. Hinge at hips, slight knee bend
5. Grip just outside legs
6. Pull back and up, not just up
7. Lock out: hips and knees together

### Bench Press Form
1. Retract shoulder blades
2. Slight arch in lower back
3. Feet flat on floor
4. Lower bar to mid-chest
5. Elbows at 45-degree angle
6. Press straight up
7. Full lockout at top

### Push-up Form
1. Hands shoulder-width
2. Body in straight line
3. Core engaged
4. Lower until chest nearly touches
5. Elbows at 45 degrees
6. Push back up fully
7. Don't let hips sag

### Pull-up Form
1. Hands slightly wider than shoulders
2. Dead hang start
3. Pull shoulder blades down first
4. Pull until chin over bar
5. Control the descent
6. Full extension at bottom
7. No swinging or kipping

## üìä Progress Tracking

### What to Track

**Workout Log:**
- Exercise name
- Weight used
- Sets and reps completed
- Rest periods
- How it felt (RPE 1-10)
- Any modifications made

**Body Measurements (Every 2 Weeks):**
- Weight
- Body fat % (if possible)
- Chest, waist, hips
- Arms, thighs
- Progress photos (same lighting/angle)

**Performance Metrics:**
- 1-Rep Max for main lifts
- Max reps at bodyweight
- Running/cycling times
- Flexibility progress

### When to Increase

**Add Weight When:**
- Complete all sets/reps with good form
- Last set doesn't feel extremely difficult
- RPE is 7 or below (out of 10)
- Same weight for 2+ consecutive workouts

**Increase Amount:**
- Upper body: 2.5-5 lbs
- Lower body: 5-10 lbs
- Machines: 5-10 lbs
- Start conservative

## üçé Nutrition Basics

### Macronutrients

**Protein (4 cal/gram):**
- 1.6-2.2g per kg body weight
- Higher when in deficit
- Spread throughout day
- Sources: chicken, fish, eggs, lean beef, Greek yogurt, protein powder

**Carbohydrates (4 cal/gram):**
- Fuel for workouts
- 2-6g per kg (depends on activity)
- Time around workouts
- Sources: rice, oats, potatoes, fruits, whole grains

**Fats (9 cal/gram):**
- 0.5-1g per kg body weight
- Essential for hormones
- Don't fear healthy fats
- Sources: avocado, nuts, olive oil, fatty fish

### Meal Timing

**Pre-Workout (1-2 hours before):**
- Carbs + Moderate protein
- Low fat, low fiber
- Example: Rice + chicken, banana + protein shake

**Post-Workout (Within 2 hours):**
- Protein + Carbs
- Fast-digesting preferred
- Example: Protein shake + fruit, chicken + rice

**Throughout Day:**
- Eat protein every 3-4 hours
- 3-5 balanced meals
- Stay consistent

### Hydration

**Daily Needs:**
- Half your body weight in ounces (minimum)
- More if training intensely
- More in hot weather
- Clear/pale yellow urine is goal

**During Workouts:**
- Sip throughout
- 7-10 oz every 10-20 minutes
- Add electrolytes if over 60 minutes

## üò¥ Recovery Strategies

### Sleep

**Why It Matters:**
- Muscle growth occurs during sleep
- Hormones regulated
- Performance improves
- Injury risk decreases

**Tips:**
- 7-9 hours per night
- Consistent schedule
- Dark, cool room
- No screens 1 hour before bed
- Consider magnesium supplement

### Active Recovery

**Best Activities:**
- Walking
- Light yoga
- Swimming
- Cycling at easy pace
- Stretching routine

**Benefits:**
- Increases blood flow
- Reduces soreness
- Maintains mobility
- Mental recovery

### Rest Days

**How Many:**
- Beginners: 3-4 per week
- Intermediate: 2-3 per week
- Advanced: 1-2 per week

**What to Do:**
- Complete rest or active recovery
- Focus on nutrition and hydration
- Work on mobility
- Plan next week's workouts

## üö´ Common Mistakes to Avoid

### Training Mistakes

1. **Ego Lifting**
   - Using too much weight
   - Sacrificing form for reps
   - Solution: Leave ego at door, focus on form

2. **Not Progressing**
   - Same weight/reps for months
   - Solution: Track workouts, follow progressive overload

3. **Overtraining**
   - Training every day
   - No rest days
   - Solution: Schedule rest, listen to body

4. **Neglecting Warm-up**
   - Jumping into heavy lifts
   - Solution: 5-10 min warm-up always

5. **Inconsistency**
   - Skipping workouts frequently
   - Solution: Schedule workouts like appointments

### Nutrition Mistakes

1. **Not Eating Enough Protein**
   - Solution: Track protein, aim for 1.6-2.2g/kg

2. **Extreme Dieting**
   - Solution: Moderate deficit, sustainable approach

3. **Neglecting Vegetables**
   - Solution: Include veggies in most meals

4. **Not Drinking Water**
   - Solution: Carry water bottle, set reminders

5. **Cheat Days**
   - Solution: Flexible dieting approach instead

## üìà Realistic Expectations

### Muscle Gain

**Beginners:**
- 1-2 lbs per month (men)
- 0.5-1 lb per month (women)
- First year: 15-20 lbs possible (men)
- First year: 8-10 lbs possible (women)

**Intermediate:**
- 0.5-1 lb per month
- Slower than beginner gains
- More focus on specific areas

**Advanced:**
- 0.25-0.5 lb per month
- Extremely slow progress
- Optimization critical

### Fat Loss

**Healthy Rate:**
- 1-2 lbs per week
- 1% body weight per week
- Faster risks muscle loss
- Slower ensures muscle preservation

**Timeline:**
- Visible changes: 4-6 weeks
- Significant changes: 12+ weeks
- Complete transformation: 6-12 months

### Strength Gains

**Beginners:**
- Can double strength in 6 months
- Linear progression possible
- Rapid neural adaptations

**Intermediate:**
- 5-10% gains per year
- Slower but steady
- Periodization helps

**Advanced:**
- 2-5% gains per year
- Very slow progress
- Every pound counts
